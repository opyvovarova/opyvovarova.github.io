about me

Who am I:

My name is Yulia Nazarenko, I am 32 years old, and I have been an oral
interpreter and interpreter coach since 2017, and a translator since 2011.

I have worked for international organizations at the highest level, as well
as NGOs and companies. Sometimes I worked in hostile and volatile
environments, together with teams of international employees.

I’ve helped to facilitate dialogue, launch companies into new markets,
and deliver their messages on various topics, including politics and
diplomatics, economy and finance, the environment, and more, including
sports and winemaking.

My mission is constant: to offer a bespoke service that caters for all types
of interpretation, or as I call it: to interpret the music of your words
without missing a note.

But why the music?

Get to know me:

I grew up in a small village in the East of Ukraine back in the 90s.
It was a formative time for me, characterised by challenges to
overcome, such as getting to school on foot, as it was located in a
different village, and balancing my different interests: diligently
attending English lessons on time, thanks to my having a bike,
and finding time to play the piano. This period contributed to
forming the aspects of my personality I am most proud of today:
being persistent and consistent, achieving results, loving
languages, my piano, and playing beautiful music.

Playing piano was pivotal for me. I wasn’t a promising student to
start out with, but my teacher, and I will forever be grateful to her,
taught me to play difficult passages, and hence to overcome
challenges and hone my assiduity, and to love classical music and
musical notation.
Since then, I have never stopped playing piano, composing songs
and performing them on stage. I believe that music in my
childhood fostered a love for the pianoforte which endures to this
day.

Now I tie all these skills into my profession — conference
interpreting and a professionalism borne of love for what I do. My
childhood echoes in me about how important it is to train my
skills every day.

So, just like a musician plays every single day, I hone my main skill:
to transfer YOUR message precisely, to sound perfect.

For me, your words are music, so I keep my expertise up to par —
to interpret the music of your words without missing a note.

How do I improve my interpreting ?

“How many times do I have to practise my piano
playing/interpreting to become a master? — “You
only need to practice on the days that you eat”

I have a daily routine for interpretation. Why? To maintain peak skills and the high level of language needed for interpreting. 20-30 minutes of simultaneous or consecutive plus vocabulary work and preparation for your event.

I study textbooks and even write posts in social media with my summaries, as books are a must for interpreters to be like an encyclopedia.

I take different learning courses almost constantly. Once one training event is over, I start another one. I truly believe that investing time and money into education is one of the unshakable pillars of being professional. Among the numerous courses I’ve attended are the following:
“Simultaneous interpretation” ( A. Falaleyev)
“English phonetics and pronunciation” (O. Glotova)
